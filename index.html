For Atlas

Why xAPI matters here

Atlas orchestrates modules, assessments, and inquiry threads—across campus and in the field. xAPI gives you a standard, append-only record of what happened: who did what, with which activities, in what context. That record is what feeds dashboards, research, and defensible credentials—not a vendor’s proprietary gradebook.

  • Interoperable — statements can move between systems without losing meaning.
  • Evidence-rich — verbs, activities, and extensions carry inquiry semantics, not just scores.
  • Future-proof — issue badges, verifiable credentials, and analytics from the same evidence stream.
Local-first

The Inquiry Appliance

Not every lab or cohort has reliable connectivity. The Inquiry Appliance is a Castalia node: a local LRS, event gateway, and sync layer that captures learning events the same way the cloud does—so notebooks, cards, and devices keep working offline, then reconcile when policy allows.

What you get on the appliance

  • Same xAPI surface as cloud — …/xAPI/statements, identical semantics.
  • Registered events only — no semantic drift; every emission maps through the gateway registry.
  • Selective sync — full evidence, credential-only, or summary modes per trust and privacy rules.
  • Durable capture — append-only log; dedupe by statement id when sync catches up.

Atlas markets this as inquiry without apology: rigorous evidence when you’re online, and the same rigor when you’re not—without two different products.
